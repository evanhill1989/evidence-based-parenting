## Understanding Corrected Age for Premature Babies

If your baby was born prematurely, you must adjust their age when comparing milestones:

**How to calculate corrected age:**

- Subtract weeks premature from their chronological age  
  _(e.g., 12-month-old born 8 weeks early â†’ corrected age 10 months)_

**When to use corrected age:**

- Use corrected age until **2 years**, sometimes up to **3 years**
- Always mention prematurity when discussing development
- Former preemies may need closer monitoring

## The Importance of Early Intervention

If your child has developmental delays, early intervention can make a tremendous difference:

**Why early matters:**

- Rapid brain development in the first 3 years
- Skills build on each other; delays can compound
- Earlier support improves long-term outcomes
- Helps parents learn effective strategies at home

**What early intervention includes:**

- Physical therapy for motor delays
- Speech therapy for communication delays
- Occupational therapy for fine motor or sensory needs
- Developmental therapy for global delays
- Family support and coaching

**Cost and access:**

- Birthâ€“3: Usually free through state Early Intervention
- Age 3+: Free services through school districts (IDEA Part B)
- Private therapy available through insurance or self-pay
